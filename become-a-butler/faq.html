About the Role

What is a VELTM Butler?

A VELTM Butler is a human travel concierge who handles real-time requests from travelers via text through the VELTM Butler Button platform. When a traveler activates their Butler service — whether a Trip Planning package, an 8-Hour day service, or a premium 24-Hour package — they are connected to a certified Butler who manages their requests, solves problems, makes reservations, coordinates logistics, and provides the attentive, personal service that defines the VELTM experience.

Unlike traditional travel agents who sell and book trips in advance, Butlers are primarily operational — working with travelers during their trips, handling live requests as they arise, and being the human presence that ensures everything goes smoothly (and recovers gracefully when it does not).

Is Butler work a job or a freelance arrangement?

Butler work is an independent contractor arrangement. You are not an employee of VELTM — you are a certified member of the Butler network who takes on engagements on a flexible, self-directed basis. You set your own availability, accept the engagements you choose, and earn per engagement rather than receiving a salary. This means full schedule flexibility but also means you are responsible for your own tax obligations as an independent contractor in your jurisdiction.

Do I serve travelers in person or remotely?

Entirely remotely. All Butler-traveler communication happens via text through the VELTM platform. You never need to meet your clients in person. You can serve travelers in Tokyo from your home in Toronto, or assist a traveler in Morocco while sitting in a coffee shop in Madrid. The service is text-based by design — it is what makes it scalable, flexible, and accessible to both travelers and Butlers worldwide.

What does a typical day as a Butler look like?

There is no single typical day — it depends entirely on the engagements you have accepted and your availability schedule. A part-time Butler might have one active 8-Hour engagement on a Saturday, responding to traveler requests throughout the day while doing other things between messages. A full-time Butler might manage two or three engagements in different stages simultaneously — one in active service, one in Trip Planning, one being closed out with a post-engagement note. Engagement intensity varies: some days are quiet with occasional requests; others are demanding and fast-paced. That variability is part of what makes the work engaging.

What happens if I cannot handle a traveler's request?

Use Butler AI to find the answer, and use the platform's escalation tools if a situation requires support beyond what you can independently resolve. No Butler is expected to know everything. What you are expected to do is find the right answer quickly, be honest when something is outside your expertise, and escalate appropriately when needed. VELTM has an operations support team available to Butlers for situations that require escalation.

Earnings and Payments

How do Butlers earn money?

Butler earnings come from two sources: a per-engagement commission on each completed engagement, and an hourly active service rate during 8-Hour and 24-Hour engagement windows. Commission rates vary by service tier and certification level. Advanced-certified Butlers with specialist designations earn premium rates on engagements that match their specialty. See the Butler Earnings Guide and Earnings Per Booking pages for detailed breakdowns.

How much can I realistically earn?

Earnings vary significantly depending on your engagement volume, service tier mix, certification level, ratings, and specialization. Part-time Butlers working 8–15 hours per week typically earn $700–$1,500 per month. Active Butlers working 20–30 hours per week earn $2,000–$3,500 per month. Full-time Butlers at the top of the platform with Advanced Certification earn $6,000–$10,000+ per month. These are estimates based on platform averages, not guarantees — actual earnings depend on your individual performance and market conditions.

When and how do I get paid?

Butler earnings are processed on a bi-weekly cycle. Payouts are made via direct bank transfer or your preferred digital payment method. There is no minimum payout threshold — all earned amounts are paid out in the next cycle. Your Butler dashboard shows real-time pending earnings, completed engagement history, and projected payout amounts.

Are there any fees to join or maintain my membership?

There is a modest annual membership fee for certified Butlers that covers platform access, AI tool usage, ongoing training materials, and community access. The base certification program is included in your membership — there is no separate training fee. Specific membership fee details are provided during the application process.

Training and Certification

What training is provided?

The VELTM Butler Certification Program is a comprehensive online training course covering platform tools, service standards, AI integration, cultural competency, emergency protocols, and the business of building a Butler practice. It is self-paced, entirely online, and completable in 2–4 weeks. The program includes a practicum — a simulated engagement with a senior Butler mentor — before you go live. See the Butler Training and Certification Program pages for full details.

How long does certification take?

Most candidates complete the base certification program in 2–4 weeks working a few hours per week. The program must be completed within 90 days of enrollment. There is no minimum time requirement — you can move faster if you have more time to dedicate. The practicum must be scheduled separately and typically takes 60–90 minutes.

Do I need prior travel or hospitality experience to get certified?

No. Prior experience in travel, hospitality, or concierge work is valuable but not required. The certification program is designed to take motivated candidates from any background and give them the skills, tools, and knowledge they need to serve clients professionally. What matters is your service orientation, communication skills, and commitment to completing the program seriously.

What is Advanced Certification and do I need it?

Advanced Certification is an optional specialist program for experienced Butlers looking to unlock higher earning rates and premium engagement matching. It is not required to participate in the network — standard certification is sufficient to start taking engagements. Advanced Certification tracks include Luxury Specialist, Group Coordination, Corporate Butler, and Destination Expert. It requires at least 20 completed engagements and a 4.7+ star rating before you can enroll. See Advanced Certification for full details.

Schedule and Availability

Can I do this part-time?

Yes — and most Butlers do, at least initially. The platform is designed for flexible, part-time participation. You control your availability calendar completely. There is no minimum number of hours or engagements required per week. Many Butlers work evenings and weekends around a full-time job, or take engagements during gaps in a freelance schedule. You can scale up or down based on your capacity at any given time.

Do I have to be available 24 hours a day?

No. You only receive engagement requests during windows when you have marked yourself as available on the platform. If you accept a 24-Hour engagement, you are committing to availability for that specific 24-hour period — but you choose whether and when to accept those engagements. You are never on call by default; you are always operating within windows you have explicitly opened.

Can I take time off whenever I want?

Yes. Close your availability calendar and no engagement requests will come through. There is no notice period required for personal time off, no approval process, and no penalty for taking breaks. The only commitment is honoring engagements you have already accepted — once you have accepted an active engagement, you are expected to see it through.

Language and Location

What languages are needed?

English fluency is required for all Butlers — it is the primary communication language on the platform for both traveler interactions and internal tools. Every additional language you speak is a significant asset. VELTM operates across 150+ languages and actively matches travelers with Butlers who share their preferred language. Butlers who speak Arabic, Mandarin, French, Spanish, Portuguese, Hindi, Japanese, and other major languages are in particularly high demand.

Does my location matter?

Your location affects which engagement types you are naturally strongest for — a Butler based in Istanbul has a built-in advantage serving travelers in Turkey — but it does not limit what you can do on the platform. Many engagements do not require geographic proximity to the traveler. What matters is your knowledge, language skills, and availability — and those travel with you wherever you are.

Can I Butler while traveling myself?

Yes, as long as you have reliable internet access and can genuinely honor the availability you have set. Many Butlers find that their own travel enriches their ability to serve clients — the fresh, direct experience of a destination feeds directly into the quality and specificity of the service you provide. The practical requirement is simply that you are reachable and responsive during your active availability windows, wherever you happen to be.

Getting Started

How do I get started?

Submit an application through the VELTM Butler network. The application takes approximately 20 minutes and covers your background, language skills, destination expertise, and motivations. Qualified applicants are invited to complete the onboarding assessment, then enrolled in the certification program. Once certified, your profile goes live and you can start accepting engagements. See Application Tips to put your best foot forward.

How long does the application process take?

Most applications are reviewed within 5–7 business days. Once approved, you can begin the certification program immediately. From application submission to your first live engagement, the typical timeline is 3–6 weeks — faster if you move through the certification program quickly, slower if you take a more relaxed pace.

What if my application is not accepted?

Not every application is accepted in the first round. If your application is not approved, you will receive feedback on why and guidance on what you could strengthen before reapplying. Many Butlers who were not accepted on their first application were approved after addressing specific gaps. Reapplication is welcome after a 90-day period.

I still have questions. Who can I contact?

Reach out to us directly at hello@veltmtours.com with any questions about the application or the Butler network. We read every message and respond within 2 business days.

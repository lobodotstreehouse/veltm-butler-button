5 Types of Requests You'll Handle as a Butler

From seamless airport pickups to midnight emergencies, VELTM Butlers are the calm, capable hands that make travel feel effortless. Here's a breakdown of the core request categories you'll master.

When a traveler presses the Butler Button, they're handing you the wheel. No two days look exactly alike — but the requests you'll receive fall into five primary categories. Understanding each one helps you respond with speed, confidence, and the quiet competence that defines a great Butler.

1. Transportation

Getting travelers from A to B is often the most time-sensitive request in the queue. Transportation spans everything from a pre-booked airport transfer to a last-minute car rental when a rental desk runs out of vehicles at 11 p.m.

  • Airport transfers: Coordinating meet-and-greet drivers, confirming flight times, handling delays, and communicating changes to ground crews across different time zones.
  • Train bookings: Reserving high-speed rail, sleeper cars, and scenic routes — often in languages the traveler doesn't speak. You'll learn the quirks of Trenitalia, Renfe, JR Pass, and more.
  • Car rentals: Matching the right vehicle class to the trip, navigating insurance requirements by country, and sourcing alternatives when primary vendors fall through.
  • Private drivers and helicopters: For premium clients, this means sourcing vetted operators, verifying licensing, and confirming itineraries to the minute.

Speed matters here. A traveler texting "My driver didn't show — I have 40 minutes to catch a flight" needs a response and a solution within minutes, not hours.

2. Dining

Food is personal. A great dining experience can define a trip; a bad one can ruin it. As a Butler, you're not just booking tables — you're curating memories.

  • Restaurant reservations: Securing tables at sought-after spots, including those that only take reservations by phone in the local language or through relationships with the maitre d'.
  • Private dining and chef's tables: Arranging exclusive experiences — from private wine cellar dinners in Bordeaux to omakase counters in Tokyo that seat six.
  • Dietary and allergy management: Communicating complex dietary needs clearly to restaurants across language barriers. This is a critical skill — mistakes here have real consequences.
  • Food tours and market experiences: Connecting travelers with authentic culinary experiences, vetted local guides, and lesser-known gems beyond the tourist circuit.
"The restaurant was fully booked for three months. Our Butler called directly, explained our situation, and secured us the best table in the house." — VELTM traveler, Rome

3. Activities and Experiences

This is where you get to play curator. Travelers want more than sightseeing — they want moments they'll describe for years. Your job is to find and arrange them.

  • Tours and guided experiences: Private guides, small-group tours, and multi-day excursions — from Machu Picchu treks to Kyoto tea ceremonies.
  • Event tickets: Opera, football, Formula 1, concerts, art fairs — sourcing legitimate tickets, often last-minute, in markets where scalping and scams are rampant.
  • Adventure activities: Hot air balloons, scuba diving, safari game drives — vetting operators for safety credentials is non-negotiable.
  • Wellness and spa: Booking treatments, thermal baths, yoga retreats, and medical wellness programs for travelers who want to restore as well as explore.

4. Accommodations

Accommodation requests often arrive at the worst moments — when plans change, arrivals get delayed, or expectations don't match reality. Butlers bridge the gap between what the property offers and what the traveler needs.

  • Last-minute hotel bookings: When a traveler's plans change mid-trip, you'll need to find availability fast — sometimes in cities during peak season or major events.
  • Room upgrades and special requests: Leveraging VELTM's supplier relationships to secure a higher floor, a specific view, or a connecting room for a family.
  • Early check-in and late checkout: Negotiating on behalf of travelers so their schedule, not the hotel's, takes priority.
  • Vacation rentals and villas: Vetting properties, coordinating key exchanges, and ensuring the listing matches what was advertised.

5. Emergency Support

This is the category that travelers remember most. When something goes wrong — and eventually it will — the Butler is the person who makes it right. This is where trust is built or broken.

  • Lost items: Coordinating with hotels, airlines, taxi companies, and local lost-and-found offices across language barriers to recover passports, luggage, devices, and valuables.
  • Medical referrals: Identifying reputable English-speaking physicians, hospitals, or clinics — and in some cases, helping arrange medical evacuation logistics alongside the traveler's insurance provider.
  • Flight rebookings: When a flight cancels, you're often the fastest path to a new itinerary — working carrier options, alliance partners, and alternate routing simultaneously.
  • Safety situations: Natural disasters, civil unrest, or personal safety concerns require calm, accurate information and rapid coordination with local contacts.

Emergency support requests are stressful — for the traveler. Your job is to be the steady, resourceful presence on the other end of that text. The VELTM platform connects you with a community of experienced Butlers and a supplier network built for exactly these moments.

What Makes a Great Butler Across All Five Categories?

The common thread is proactive communication. Travelers don't want to wonder whether you received their request or whether you're working on it. Acknowledge quickly, update often, and confirm every detail before signing off.

Butler Smart Recommendations

VELTM's AI-powered recommendation engine does the research so you can focus on the relationship. Here's how Smart Recommendations works and how to get the most out of it.

One of the most time-consuming parts of being a great Butler is research. A traveler asks for "a memorable dinner in Kyoto" and suddenly you're sifting through dozens of options, trying to match restaurant style, price point, cuisine type, and availability to a person you may have only communicated with briefly. Smart Recommendations changes that equation.

What Smart Recommendations Does

Smart Recommendations is an AI-powered tool built into the VELTM Butler platform. It analyzes the traveler's profile, trip context, and stated preferences to generate a curated shortlist of experiences, properties, restaurants, and activities — ranked by match quality and annotated with the reasons each option was selected.

  • Contextual awareness: The tool considers destination, travel dates, party composition (solo, couple, family, group), budget tier, and any past preferences on record for the traveler.
  • Real-time availability: Recommendations are filtered for availability during the traveler's dates, so you're not presenting options that can't be booked.
  • Preference learning: Over time, as the traveler accepts or declines recommendations and provides feedback, the system refines its model. Repeat travelers get increasingly personalized suggestions.
  • Butler override: You can always override, reorder, or remove suggestions before presenting them to a traveler. The AI is a starting point, not a final answer — your judgment and local knowledge always take precedence.

How to Use the Tool

Smart Recommendations is accessible from any active traveler request. Here's the typical workflow:

  1. Open the request context panel: When you receive a new request, the context panel shows the traveler's profile, trip details, and any prior history. This is the data the recommendation engine draws from.
  2. Trigger the recommendation run: Click "Get Smart Recommendations" and specify the request category (dining, accommodation, activity, transportation) and any additional filters — cuisine type, price range, accessibility requirements.
  3. Review the shortlist: The tool returns a ranked list of typically 5–10 options with match scores and reasoning. Each option links to booking details, supplier contact information, and any VELTM partner rates applicable.
  4. Apply your expertise: This is where your value as a human Butler is irreplaceable. You might know that the top-ranked restaurant has had a recent chef change, or that a highly rated hotel is undergoing renovation noise. Layer your knowledge over the AI output.
  5. Present to the traveler: Share a curated selection — typically three options with a clear recommendation — in a format that's easy for the traveler to respond to. The platform's recommendation share feature formats this automatically.

Personalization Features

The more a traveler uses VELTM, the more powerful Smart Recommendations becomes. Several personalization layers work in the background:

  • Preference tags: Travelers can explicitly set preferences (e.g., "prefers boutique hotels," "vegetarian," "avoids tourist crowds") that are factored into every recommendation run.
  • Behavioral signals: When a traveler consistently accepts recommendations in a certain category — say, design-forward properties or outdoor adventure activities — the system notes this and weights future suggestions accordingly.
  • Exclusion memory: Properties, restaurant chains, or experience types a traveler has explicitly declined are excluded from future suggestions. You won't be recommending the same Marriott property to someone who told you they avoid chain hotels.
  • Trip type context: The system distinguishes between a business trip and a honeymoon, a solo adventure and a family holiday. The same traveler will receive meaningfully different recommendations depending on trip type.

When Not to Rely on It

Smart Recommendations is a powerful tool, but it has limits every Butler should understand:

  • Hyper-local or emerging spots: The tool draws from established supplier databases. The tiny new natural wine bar that opened last month and is already booked out — the one a local friend told you about — won't be in the system yet. Your human network fills this gap.
  • Complex emotional context: A traveler who's returning to a city where they got engaged, or who wants to recreate a trip they took with a deceased parent, needs human sensitivity in the recommendation, not an algorithm.
  • Last-minute emergencies: When availability is the only constraint and you need a room in the next hour, a direct supplier call will always be faster than a recommendation engine.

Think of Smart Recommendations as the world's most well-read research assistant — incredibly fast, well-organized, and deeply knowledgeable. But you're the Butler. Your empathy, judgment, and relationships make the difference between a good suggestion and the right one.

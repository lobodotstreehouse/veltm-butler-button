Managing Your Butler Availability

One of the most important things you control as a VELTM Butler is when you work. Here's how to set your availability, manage time zone complexity, and use VELTM's scheduling tools to build a sustainable practice.

VELTM Butlers are independent professionals. You decide when you work, how many clients you take on, and what types of requests you specialize in. The availability system exists to give travelers accurate expectations and to match them with the right Butler at the right time — not to impose a schedule on you.

Used well, it's one of the most powerful tools in the platform.

Setting Your Available Hours

Your availability schedule is the foundation of your Butler profile. Travelers and the VELTM matching system use it to determine whether you can take a request right now or whether they should be routed to another Butler.

  • Be honest, not optimistic: Set the hours you can reliably respond within 15 minutes — not the hours you might theoretically be awake. A traveler who texts at 2 p.m. expecting a response and doesn't hear back for two hours has a worse experience than one who was matched with an available Butler from the start.
  • Create distinct weekday and weekend schedules: Most Butlers find their availability naturally differs. The platform supports separate weekday and weekend windows — use them.
  • Block time for focused work: Research, itinerary building, and supplier negotiations require concentration. Block these as "deep work" periods where you're not accepting new inbound requests but are actively serving existing clients.
  • Update proactively: Going on holiday yourself? Attending a family event? Update your availability before the period begins, not after a traveler has already texted expecting a response.

Managing Time Zones

Travel is inherently global. A Butler based in London may be serving a traveler currently in Singapore and planning a trip to Brazil. Time zone management is a daily operational reality.

  • Display your hours in multiple zones: Your VELTM profile shows your availability in the traveler's local time zone automatically. Keep your own schedule in your home time zone — the platform handles the conversion.
  • Understand your peak request windows: Most urgent travel requests come in during the traveler's local business hours and early evening. If you specialize in a particular region, align at least part of your availability window with those peak hours.
  • Use the time zone as an advantage: A Butler based in Southeast Asia naturally covers hours that are nighttime in Europe and the Americas — making them invaluable for travelers who need coverage during those windows. Lean into this rather than fighting it.
  • Set expectations for response times across zones: If a traveler in a distant time zone sends a non-urgent request during your off hours, the platform's auto-response feature will notify them of your next available window. Configure this message to be warm and specific.

The Scheduling System: How It Works

VELTM's scheduling system does more than display your hours. It actively manages the flow of requests to ensure travelers get responses and Butlers don't get overwhelmed.

  • Real-time availability toggle: The green/amber/red status indicator in your Butler dashboard lets you signal your current responsiveness in real time. Green means you're available for new requests now. Amber means you're handling existing requests and response may be slightly delayed. Red means you're unavailable and new requests will be routed elsewhere.
  • Capacity limits: Set a maximum number of active client engagements you'll handle simultaneously. The system will stop routing new requests to you once that limit is reached, even during your available hours.
  • Advance booking windows: Travelers can see your availability and send requests up to 90 days in advance. This allows you to take on trip planning work well ahead of departure — often the most valuable part of the Butler engagement.
  • Handoff protocols: When a request comes in during your off hours or when you're at capacity, VELTM's system can route it to a covering Butler. You can designate preferred cover colleagues — useful for maintaining continuity with travelers you've served before.

Building a Schedule That Works for Your Life

The best Butler schedules are ones that are sustainable for months and years, not just weeks. Some patterns that work well:

  • The specialist window: Some Butlers set narrow but deeply reliable availability — say, 8 a.m. to 6 p.m. in one time zone — and build a reputation for being rock-solid within that window. Consistency earns trust faster than breadth.
  • The coverage specialist: Some Butlers choose to be available during hours that most others aren't — overnight, early morning, or on weekends — and become the go-to resource for those windows.
  • The project model: Rather than maintaining daily availability, some experienced Butlers accept trip planning engagements in advance and are intensively available during the client's actual travel dates, with limited availability between trips.

There is no single right answer. The platform is flexible enough to support all of these models. What matters is that your availability settings accurately reflect your actual working pattern — because that's what travelers and the matching system depend on.

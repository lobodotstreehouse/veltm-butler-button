How much it realistically costs to plan a trip and how it compares to travel agents

Butler Button is designed to be affordable for everyday travelers, not just people with big budgets. Trip planning starts at $25 per country. Most people spend somewhere between $75 and $300 to plan a full vacation, and many find that the hotel deals we unlock more than cover that cost.

Traditional travel agents often charge a percentage of your total trip cost, which can add up to hundreds or thousands of dollars depending on what you are booking. Butler Button uses flat, transparent fees instead. You pay for planning and, if you want it, day-of support. No percentage cuts. No commissions baked into your hotel rate.

Because we have partner relationships with 500 or more hotels, we can get you rates that are 10 to 59 percent below what you would pay booking directly or through a standard travel site. For most trips, those savings are larger than the planning fee itself.

A Realistic Budget Breakdown

Here is what a typical trip might look like cost-wise on the Butler Button side:

  • One-country vacation, planning only: $25
  • Two-country trip, planning only: $50
  • Two-country trip with one Butler day added: $75 to $125
  • Full multi-country trip with a mix of Butler days: $150 to $300

These are the fees you pay Butler Button. Your actual travel costs (flights, hotels, activities) are separate and paid directly to those providers.

You Only Pay for What You Use

You are never required to buy a full package. If you only need a plan for one country, you pay $25. If you want Butler support for one busy travel day, you add that. The structure is modular, so your budget goes exactly where you want it.

Total Butler Button cost: $50.

What Is a Concierge?

What a travel concierge does and how Butler Button handles your trip from start to finish

A concierge is someone who handles the details of your trip so you don't have to. That means getting the hard-to-land dinner reservation, figuring out what to do when your flight gets cancelled, or tracking down a trusted local guide in a city you've never set foot in. A good concierge saves you time, stress, and the kind of mistakes that come from not knowing a place.

What Kind of Things Does a Concierge Handle?

  • Booking restaurants, tours, and experiences in advance
  • Arranging drivers, transfers, and airport pickups
  • Rebooking flights and hotels when plans change
  • Finding local doctors, pharmacies, or emergency services when needed
  • Chasing down lost luggage with the airline on your behalf
  • Pulling off surprises for birthdays, anniversaries, or proposals

How Butler Button Works as Your Travel Concierge

Butler Button is a travel concierge service from VELTM Tours. We handle your trip in three stages: planning it before you go, running it while you're there, and fixing problems as they come up. You get a real person, not just a chatbot, who is responsible for making sure your trip goes well.

Our service combines AI tools that can scan hundreds of thousands of options in seconds with human Butlers who make the judgment calls, pick up the phone, and deal with situations that don't fit a script.

Tip: If you're not sure whether something is Butler work, ask anyway. If it's travel-related and it could ruin your day, we want to know about it.

Isn't This Just a Hotel Concierge?

How Butler Button differs from a hotel concierge and why having support for your whole trip matters

A hotel concierge is helpful, but their job ends at the front desk. Once you check out, you're on your own. Butler Button goes with you for the whole trip, airports, transfers, restaurants, tours, and everything in between. We don't clock out when you leave the lobby.

What a Hotel Concierge Can and Can't Do

Hotel concierges are good at a specific set of things:

  • Recommending nearby restaurants and attractions
  • Calling a cab or arranging a hotel car service
  • Holding your luggage after checkout
  • Answering questions about the local area

What they generally can't do:

  • Help you before you arrive at the hotel
  • Rebook your flight when it gets cancelled at 11 p.m.
  • Coordinate between multiple hotels on a multi-city trip
  • Help you at the airport, during a transfer, or after you've checked out

Your Butler Stays With You

With Butler Button, your assigned Butler is your single point of contact for the entire trip. It doesn't matter where you are in the itinerary. If you're on day one in one city and day seven in a completely different region, you call the same person. They already know your trip, your preferences, and what matters to you.

Tip: If your trip covers more than one hotel or more than one city, a hotel concierge can only help with the part of the trip they're responsible for. Butler Button handles the whole picture.

How Long Should My Trip Be?

Trip length guidance for city breaks, multi-country travel, and road trips

The right trip length depends on your destination, your pace, and what you want to get out of the travel. As a general rule, three to five days works well for a single city, and seven to fourteen days gives you room to breathe on a multi-city or multi-country trip.

City Breaks: 3 to 5 Days

A long weekend or four to five days is usually enough to get a real feel for one city without burning out on sightseeing. Going shorter than three days often means you spend most of it jet-lagged or rushing.

Multi-City or Country Trips: 7 to 14 Days

When you are crossing borders or moving between major destinations, you need time to account for travel days. Fourteen days lets you move at a comfortable pace and actually settle into each place rather than just passing through.

Matching Trip Length to Your Travel Style

  • Relaxed traveler: Add at least one buffer day per four days of travel.
  • Active traveler: You can pack more in, but recovery days matter on longer trips.
  • Traveling with kids or older adults: Build in more downtime than you think you need.
Tip: A common mistake is adding one more destination without adding enough time. Your Butler will be honest with you if the pacing feels off.

Can Butler Button Plan Family Trips?

Planning family vacations with kids, mixed ages, and different activity preferences

Yes, and family trips are one of our most common requests. We build itineraries that work for the whole group, not just the adults, and we find hotels that genuinely work for kids rather than ones that just check a box on a booking site.

What Makes a Family Trip Different

  • Kids and adults want different things from the same afternoon
  • "Family-friendly" hotel labels mean almost nothing without knowing the property
  • Meal timing and food options matter a lot more than on adult-only trips
  • Safety and neighborhood feel carry more weight when children are involved

Hotels That Actually Work for Families

  • Room configurations that fit more than two people without paying for two separate rooms

Traditional agents typically earn money through supplier commissions. When they book you a hotel, that hotel often pays the agent a percentage of the room rate.

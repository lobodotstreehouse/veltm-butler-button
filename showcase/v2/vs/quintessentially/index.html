Frequently Asked

Butler Button vs. Quintessentially.

What's the difference between Butler Button and Quintessentially? +

Quintessentially is a private members-only lifestyle concierge. It covers a broad surface — travel, dining, events, luxury goods, personal services — via annual membership.

Butler Button is a travel-focused concierge offered pay-per-use at $25 per day of service, with no membership required. Butler Button's parent, VELTM Tours, has served heads of state and royal families of Saudi Arabia and the UAE since 2019 — delivering private-tier travel access without the membership gate.

How much does Quintessentially cost? +

Quintessentially does not publish membership pricing; rates are provided on application and vary by tier. Widely reported figures place entry-tier memberships around US$4,000 per year, with Elite and by-invitation tiers reaching tens of thousands of dollars annually.

Butler Button is $25 per day of concierge service or $25 per country for trip planning. No annual fee. No application. No referral.

Can Butler Button access the same hotels, flights, and reservations? +

Yes. Butler Button operates 500+ direct hotel partnerships, private aviation charter relationships, and restaurant networks — built over years of serving royal families, heads of state, and ultra-high-net-worth travelers.

The relationships are equivalent. The pricing model is different: pay-per-trip instead of an annual membership fee.

Does Butler Button require an application or referral? +

No. Butler Button is open to any traveler. Unlike traditional private members' concierges, there is no application form, no vetting process, and no referral requirement. Book a trip and your advisor starts work.

Is Butler Button a lifestyle concierge? +

No. Butler Button is focused specifically on travel: itinerary planning, bookings, in-trip support, and disruption handling. It does not cover broader lifestyle services (daily dining reservations unrelated to travel, ticketed events outside a trip, personal shopping, etc.).

For travel specifically, the access Butler Button delivers is comparable to a private members' travel desk. For full-spectrum lifestyle coverage, a members' concierge is the right product.

Who are Butler Button's clients? +

VELTM Tours — Butler Button's parent company — has served heads of state, royal families of Saudi Arabia and the UAE, ultra-high-net-worth travelers, and celebrities since 2019. That experience built the partnerships and operating standards Butler Button uses today.

Butler Button opens the same model and access to any traveler, at $25 per day. Discretion is unchanged: 100% independent, no affiliate agreements, private clientele handled by referral at the firm level.

Does Butler Button handle private aviation? +

Yes. Butler Button has direct relationships with private jet charter operators and luxury air carriers. Your advisor coordinates private aviation as part of your itinerary — sourcing, booking, ground coordination, and real-time schedule management.

Can I use my existing credit card with Butler Button and still earn points? +

Yes. Butler Button is card-agnostic. You tell us which card to charge and we use it for all bookings — the $25/day service fee plus every hotel, excursion, and transport booking. You earn whatever points, miles, or cash back your card offers.

You can also share your rewards goals at checkout — earn points, redeem points, or a hybrid strategy — and your advisor will design your itinerary around your preferred program.

How does Butler Button get prices 30–40% below OTA rates? +

Butler Button's operations center is based in India. When advisors purchase hotels, excursions, and ground transport on your behalf, they do so from Indian IP addresses. Suppliers offer local market pricing at those addresses — rather than the inflated rates that western OTAs push on first-world consumers.

The average saving is 30–40% on hotels, excursions, and ground transport. Air and sea rates are globally priced and not affected.

What is Butler Button's commission structure? +

Butler Button operates a no-commission travel agency for clients who pay directly: 0% commission if you pay for travel upfront or on arrival.

If you prefer to be invoiced after your trip, the commission is 10% — below the industry standard of 12–15%. Most clients choose upfront or on-arrival payment to avoid commission entirely.

Does Butler Button donate to charity? +

Yes. Butler Button automatically donates 1% of every purchase to a nonprofit of your choice, powered by our Pledge integration. At checkout you simply pick the beneficiary from over a million registered nonprofits.

No extra steps. No extra cost to you. Butler Button absorbs the donation.

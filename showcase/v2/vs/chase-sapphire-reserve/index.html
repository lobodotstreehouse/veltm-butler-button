Butler Button and Chase Sapphire Reserve solve different problems. Butler Button is a dedicated travel concierge at $25/day. Sapphire Reserve is a $795/year premium card optimized for points, lounge access, and travel credits.

Butler Button

Pay only for days you travel. One dedicated advisor who knows your itinerary. 4-minute average response. No annual fee, no points system, cancel before delivery for a full refund.

Chase Sapphire Reserve

$795 annual fee. Points-first value proposition (up to 8x on Chase Travel). Priority Pass, $300 travel credit, and Visa Infinite Concierge (shared agent pool) bundled in.

Best for Butler Button: travelers who want a real person who already knows the trip, without paying an annual fee or chasing points.

Who Picks Which

Honest verdict.

Different problems, different answers. Here's when each wins.

Pick Butler Button if…

You want a travel expert on your trip, not a multiplier on your spending.

  • You want one advisor who has read your itinerary before you fly.
  • You don't want to pay an annual fee for a service you may not use.
  • You value real-time in-trip problem-solving over points-per-dollar.
  • You want to pay with whatever card earns you the best points anyway.
  • You take 1–5 trips per year and want predictable per-trip pricing.
Pick Sapphire Reserve if…

You're a points optimizer who maximizes card benefits across the year.

  • You book travel through Chase Travel and will use the 8x multiplier.
  • You'll fully use the $300 travel credit and Priority Pass access.
  • You want trip delay and cancellation insurance baked into your card.
  • You're comfortable working with a shared concierge line when needed.
  • You prefer one premium card covering multiple travel benefits.

Frequently Asked

Butler Button vs. Sapphire Reserve.

Does Chase Sapphire Reserve include a travel concierge? +

Yes. As a Visa Infinite card, Sapphire Reserve includes 24/7 Visa Infinite Concierge — a shared concierge team that handles requests across all Visa Infinite cardholders. It runs through Visa's concierge provider, not Chase directly.

Butler Button's model is different: one advisor per trip, who reviews your full itinerary before departure. First contact goes to a human who already knows your schedule.

How much does Butler Button cost compared to Sapphire Reserve? +

Butler Button is $25 per day of use, with no membership and no annual fee. Chase Sapphire Reserve is $795 per year as of 2026, regardless of travel frequency.

For one 10-day trip, Butler Button's concierge cost is roughly $275. Sapphire Reserve's annual fee is $795, offset to about $495 net after the $300 travel credit — plus whatever value you extract from points and Priority Pass.

Is Butler Button a replacement for Sapphire Reserve? +

No. Sapphire Reserve is a premium credit card optimized for points and bundled travel benefits. Butler Button is a standalone travel concierge service.

Many Butler Button clients also carry Sapphire Reserve — using the card to earn points on bookings, and Butler Button for the dedicated advisor who handles the trip. You can pay the $25/day fee with your Sapphire Reserve and earn Ultimate Rewards on the charge.

Does Sapphire Reserve's concierge have a dedicated advisor? +

No. Visa Infinite Concierge is a shared service — each request routes to whichever agent is available. The agent does not have your itinerary loaded in advance.

Butler Button pairs you with one advisor per trip. That advisor reads your itinerary before you depart and handles all in-trip requests directly, so you're never starting from zero.

How does points value compare? +

Chase Sapphire Reserve earns Ultimate Rewards points — up to 8x on Chase Travel bookings, 3x on dining and travel — redeemable for travel at an elevated rate. For high spenders, that can add meaningful value.

Butler Button doesn't issue points. It's a service, not a card. But you can pay the $25/day fee and all underlying travel costs with Sapphire Reserve and earn points on those charges — you don't have to choose.

Which is better for infrequent travelers? +

Butler Button. For one to three trips per year, paying $25 per day used typically costs much less than the $795 Sapphire Reserve annual fee (~$495 net after the travel credit).

Sapphire Reserve's math works best for frequent travelers who fully use the travel credit, book through Chase Travel, and regularly use Priority Pass lounges.

Does Butler Button cover trip disruption? +

Yes — operationally. Butler Button's advisor handles real-time disruption: rebooking flights, finding hotels, securing replacement reservations. 97% of in-trip disruptions are resolved in under 60 minutes.

Sapphire Reserve provides trip cancellation and delay insurance as a card benefit — reimbursement for covered conditions. Those are complementary: Butler Button handles the logistics in the moment; card insurance may reimburse you afterward.

Can I use my Chase Sapphire Reserve to pay and still earn Ultimate Rewards? +

Yes. Butler Button is card-agnostic. You tell us which card to charge and we use it for all bookings — the $25/day service fee plus every hotel, excursion, and transport booking. Using your Chase Sapphire Reserve earns 3x Ultimate Rewards on travel purchases on every transaction.

You can also share your rewards goals at checkout — earn points, redeem points, or a hybrid strategy — and your advisor will design your itinerary and booking sequence around your UR program.

How does Butler Button get prices 30–40% below OTA rates? +

Butler Button's operations center is based in India. When advisors purchase hotels, excursions, and ground transport on your behalf, they do so from Indian IP addresses. Suppliers offer local market pricing at those addresses — rather than the inflated rates that western OTAs push on first-world consumers.

The average saving is 30–40% on hotels, excursions, and ground transport. Air and sea rates are globally priced and not affected.

What is Butler Button's commission structure? +

Butler Button operates a no-commission travel agency for clients who pay directly: 0% commission if you pay for travel upfront or on arrival.

If you prefer to be invoiced after your trip, the commission is 10% — below the industry standard of 12–15%. Most clients choose upfront or on-arrival payment to avoid commission entirely.

Does Butler Button donate to charity? +

Yes. Butler Button automatically donates 1% of every purchase to a nonprofit of your choice, powered by our Pledge integration. At checkout you simply pick the beneficiary from over a million registered nonprofits.

No extra steps. No extra cost to you. Butler Button absorbs the donation.

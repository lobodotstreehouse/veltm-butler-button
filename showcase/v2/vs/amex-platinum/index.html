Who Picks Which

Honest verdict.

These products solve different problems. Here's when each wins.

Pick Butler Button if…

You want a travel expert on your trip, not a card on your wallet.

  • You take 1–5 trips per year and don't want to pay for benefits you won't use.
  • You want a dedicated advisor who has already studied your itinerary.
  • You want real-time help when a flight cancels at 11 p.m. in Bangkok.
  • You want to keep your existing cards and stack the points on your bookings.
  • You value a measured 4-minute response time over generic concierge lines.
Pick Amex Platinum if…

You're a high-frequency traveler who will use the bundled perks.

  • You fly 10+ times per year and will use Centurion Lounge regularly.
  • You'll use the full $1,500+ in annual statement credits (airline, hotel, Uber, Digital).
  • You want to earn 5x Membership Rewards on flights and prepaid hotels.
  • You prefer a single bundled product over separate services.
  • You don't need a dedicated advisor who knows your specific trip in advance.

Frequently Asked

Butler Button vs. Amex Platinum.

Does Amex Platinum include a travel concierge? +

Yes. The American Express Platinum Card includes 24/7 Platinum Travel Service and Platinum Concierge as bundled card benefits. Service is delivered through Amex's concierge team and is shared across all cardholders globally.

Butler Button's model is different: one dedicated advisor per trip, who reviews your full itinerary before you depart, so first contact goes to someone who already knows your schedule.

How much does Butler Button cost compared to Amex Platinum? +

Butler Button is $25 per day of use, with no membership and no annual fee. Amex Platinum is $895 per year regardless of how often you travel (US consumer card, as of 2026).

For a traveler taking one 8-day trip per year, Butler Button's concierge cost is roughly $200. Amex Platinum's annual fee is $895 — offset partially by statement credits and points if you use them.

Is Butler Button a replacement for Amex Platinum? +

No. Amex Platinum is a premium credit card with rewards, lounge access, statement credits, and a bundled concierge. Butler Button is a standalone travel concierge service.

Many Butler Button clients also carry Amex Platinum or a similar premium card — using the card for lounge access and points, and Butler Button for the dedicated advisor and in-trip support. The two are complementary.

Does Butler Button book flights and hotels directly? +

Yes. Butler Button advisors book flights, hotels, private aviation, restaurants, and ground transport directly with partners. There is no separate booking portal and no points requirement.

You pay for the travel itself with any card you choose — advisors will use your card to earn points on the bookings they make on your behalf.

Can I use Butler Button alongside my Amex Platinum card? +

Yes. Butler Button is payment-method agnostic. You can use your Amex Platinum to pay for the $25/day service fee and all underlying travel bookings, earning Membership Rewards points on those charges just as you would with any other Amex transaction.

Which is better for infrequent travelers? +

Butler Button. If you take one to three trips per year, paying $25 per day used is typically significantly less than the $895 Amex Platinum annual fee — and you avoid paying for lounge access, statement credits, and other benefits you may not use.

Amex Platinum's value scales with usage: the more you fly, use Centurion Lounges, and redeem statement credits, the better the fee math becomes.

What's Butler Button's average response time? +

Four minutes. Every Butler Button advisor studies your full itinerary before you depart, so your first message goes to a human who already knows your destination, your schedule, and what matters on your trip. 97% of in-trip disruptions are resolved in under 60 minutes.

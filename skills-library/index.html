What this is

Overview

12 skills installed in Claude Code, each encoding dozens of proven marketing principles from classic theory.

Claude Code's Skills system (visible in the Customize panel) lets you install persistent knowledge files that Claude references automatically when you invoke them. Each of the 12 skills in this library maps to a marketing discipline and contains the synthesized principles extracted from the source textbooks below.

These were built for the VELTM Butler Button launch — a $25/day personal travel concierge. The extraction methodology was exhaustive: all 1,206 pages of all six books were read cover-to-cover, every principle was coded, and the 391 non-overlapping skills were organized into 12 discipline files.

📖

How They Were Built

Full-text extraction from 6 textbooks using structured coding: each principle received a name, source citation, description, and a specific Butler Button application.

🔧

Where They Live

Installed at ~/.claude/skills/marketing-*/SKILL.md. Each file is a structured Markdown document that Claude Code's Customize panel surfaces as a named skill.

🔎

How to Use Them

Open Claude Code. Navigate to Customize. Find any marketing-* skill. Enable it for a session, or reference it by name in your prompt to apply that discipline's principles.

Brand Strategy & Positioning
35 principles — Ogilvy, Confessions, Bly
marketing-brand-strategy

  • Big Idea Development (5-Test Method) Ogilvy on Advertising — gasps, uniqueness, strategy fit, 30-year sustainability, emotional resonance

Headline Writing
30 principles — Ogilvy, Bly, Swipe File
marketing-headline-writing
  • 5x Multiplier: Headlines Read More Than Body Copy Ogilvy on Advertising — if the headline fails, the ad fails regardless of the copy beneath it
  • The 8 Headline Types (Bly's Classification) The Copywriter's Handbook — Direct, Indirect, News, How-To, Question, Command, Reason-Why, Testimonial
  • The 4 U's Scoring Formula The Copywriter's Handbook — Useful, Urgent, Ultra-specific, Unique; score 1-4 on each before publishing
  • Long Headlines (10+ Words) Outsell Short Ogilvy on Advertising — specificity and completeness outweigh brevity
  • Quotation Headlines Recall +28% Ogilvy on Advertising — headlines framed as quotes are recalled significantly more
When to use Writing any homepage headline, ad copy, email subject line, or social post. Write 25+ variations before selecting; apply 4 U's before publishing.
3
Body Copy & Persuasion
45 principles — Ogilvy, Bly, Confessions, Swipe File
marketing-body-copy
  • AIDA Framework (Non-Negotiable) The Copywriter's Handbook, Bly — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action; no page skips a step
  • Feature-to-Benefit Conversion The Copywriter's Handbook, Bly — readers buy outcomes, not specifications; every feature rewritten as a result
  • Problem-Agitate-Solve (PAS) Structure The $100M Swipe File — 20% problem statement, 40% agitation, 40% solution
  • Assumed-Objection Technique The $100M Swipe File — name and pre-empt the reader's doubt before they can form it
  • Long Copy Outsells Short for Considered Purchases Ogilvy, Benson, Bly — 350+ word ads outperform shorter ones when the buyer is making a real decision
When to use Writing product pages, landing pages, email sequences, or any copy longer than a headline. Reference for tone, structure, and persuasion architecture.

Visual Design & Layout
28 principles — Ogilvy, Confessions, Bly, Swipe File
marketing-visual-design
  • Photos Outperform Illustrations in Every Test Ogilvy on Advertising, Confessions of an Advertising Man — more believable, more memorable, more persuasive
  • Captions Read 2x More Than Body Copy Ogilvy on Advertising — every caption is a miniature ad; always include brand name and a selling message
  • Never Reverse Type for Body Copy Ogilvy on Advertising — white on dark backgrounds kills readership; long copy always dark-on-light
  • Editorial Layouts Get 50% More Readers Ogilvy on Advertising, Swipe File — pages that look like magazine articles outperform pages that look like ads
  • Two-Typeface Maximum Ogilvy on Advertising — consistency projects professionalism; every third typeface undermines it
When to use Designing web pages, ad creatives, email templates, or any visual marketing asset. Reference before approving any layout or image selection.

Direct Response & Conversion
55 principles — Benson, Ogilvy, Bly
marketing-direct-response
  • The List Is Most Important (Quality Over Volume) Secrets of Successful Direct Mail, Benson — 500 high-intent prospects outperform 50,000 cold followers
  • Follow-Up Pulls 50% of Original Response Secrets of Successful Direct Mail, Benson — a second contact two weeks later harvests half the original results
  • Never Sell Two Things at Once Secrets of Successful Direct Mail, Benson — one CTA per page; split focus halves response
  • Premiums Beat Price Discounts Dollar-for-Dollar Secrets of Successful Direct Mail, Benson — a $5 premium lifts orders more than a $5 price reduction
  • Risk Reversal / Guarantee Removes Buyer Anxiety The $100M Swipe File, The Copywriter's Handbook — absorb the buyer's risk explicitly, prominently, near the CTA
When to use Designing any page or communication that asks someone to take action. CTA design, offer architecture, email sequences, conversion rate optimization.

TV & Radio Advertising
25 principles — Ogilvy, Confessions, Bly
marketing-tv-radio
  • First 5 Seconds Are Critical — Grab or Lose Ogilvy on Advertising — the hook must appear in 3 seconds; viewers decide immediately whether to keep watching
  • Brand Name in First 10 Seconds Ogilvy on Advertising — display and speak the brand name immediately; repeat at close
  • Demonstrations Are Most Persuasive Ogilvy on Advertising — show the product doing what you claim; authenticity of demonstration beats any claim
  • Production Cost Negatively Correlates with Effectiveness Ogilvy on Advertising — expensive production does not predict better sales; authenticity beats polish
  • Radio: Brand Name Early and Often (4 Rules) Ogilvy on Advertising — brand early, brand often, benefit early, benefit often; name within 5 seconds
When to use Planning or scripting video ads, podcast sponsorships, short-form video content, or any audio-visual marketing. Apply before production begins.
7
Frequency & Media Planning
55 principles — Effective Frequency (Naples, Krugman)
marketing-frequency-media
  • 3-Exposure Threshold (Krugman's Law) Effective Frequency, Naples — 1st exposure: "what is it?"; 2nd: "what of it?"; 3rd: decision. Below 3, money is wasted.
  • Frequency Over Reach with Limited Budget Effective Frequency, Naples — 5,000 people at 3 exposures produces results; 15,000 at 1 exposure produces nothing
  • Wearout Is a Copy Problem, Not a Frequency Problem Effective Frequency, Naples — rotate ads only when performance data shows decline, not when you are personally bored
  • Ebbinghaus Forgetting Curve: 7-Day Maximum Gap Effective Frequency, Naples — after 7 days without a touchpoint, the prospect has largely forgotten the brand
  • New Brands Require More Frequency Than Established Ones Effective Frequency, Naples — unknown brands need 4-5 exposures minimum to achieve what known brands achieve at 3
When to use Planning any paid media campaign. Budget allocation, channel selection, retargeting window settings, and reporting (report 3+ exposure reach, not raw reach).

Offer Architecture & Pricing
38 principles — Benson, Swipe File, Bly
marketing-offer-pricing
  • Tiered Price Anchoring (Three-Tier Logic) Secrets of Successful Direct Mail, Benson — the premium tier makes the middle tier look like a bargain
  • Believable Reason for the Deal Doubles Response Secrets of Successful Direct Mail, Benson — a credible explanation multiplies the effect of the offer itself
  • Cost-Per-Day Reframing (Drip Pricing) The $100M Swipe File — compare daily cost to something the buyer already spends without thinking
  • Charter / Founder Pricing Creates Exclusivity + Urgency Secrets of Successful Direct Mail, Benson — "founding member" framing combines scarcity, special status, and time pressure
  • Always Probe Higher Prices (Most Companies Never Test) Secrets of Successful Direct Mail, Benson — a modest price increase across a customer base compounds significantly
When to use Setting or revising pricing, building a pricing page, designing an offer, writing a checkout page, or planning a promotional campaign.

Research & Testing
28 principles — Ogilvy, Benson, Confessions, Naples
marketing-research-testing
  • Research Determines the Promise, Not the Execution Ogilvy on Advertising — research reveals what to claim; craft reveals how to say it
  • Never Stop Testing (Permanent Discipline) Ogilvy on Advertising, Benson — testing is not a launch phase; it is an ongoing operating practice
  • Test Complete Packages, Not Individual Elements Secrets of Successful Direct Mail, Benson — elements interact; complete-page variants produce cleaner data than element swaps
  • Research Is Misused When It Confirms Preconceptions Ogilvy on Advertising — accept results that contradict assumptions; that is the only honest use of data
  • Measure Effect on Sales, Not Awareness Ogilvy on Advertising — the only test that matters is whether people buy; all other metrics are proxies
When to use Before writing any major campaign, after 30 paid cases (recalibration checkpoint), when designing A/B tests, when interpreting analytics, or when a campaign underperforms.

Customer Lifecycle & Retention
32 principles — Benson (Secrets of Successful Direct Mail)
marketing-customer-lifecycle
  • Two-Time Buyer = 2x Likely to Buy Again Secrets of Successful Direct Mail, Benson — the first-to-second conversion is the most important in the customer lifecycle
  • Calculate the Bogey (Maximum Allowable Acquisition Cost) Secrets of Successful Direct Mail, Benson — know lifetime value before setting acquisition budgets
  • Open-End Billing Increases Longevity 125% Secrets of Successful Direct Mail, Benson — customer must cancel rather than renew; inertia is a retention asset
  • Product Quality Determines Retention, Not Acquisition Method Secrets of Successful Direct Mail, Benson — no copy can compensate for bad service; fix operations before retention marketing
  • Reactivation: Former Customers Respond Far Better Than Cold Prospects Secrets of Successful Direct Mail, Benson — dormant customers who liked the product are the highest-ROI reactivation segment
When to use Designing post-purchase email sequences, calculating lifetime value, planning retention campaigns, building upgrade paths, or deciding how much to spend on acquisition.

Proven Swipe File Patterns
40 principles — The $100M Swipe File (Hopkins, Caples, Schwartz, Halbert)
marketing-swipe-file
  • Product-Name-Plus-Price Headline Formula The $100M Swipe File — state what it is and what it costs directly in the headline; the most reliable format across 130 pages of examples
  • Ingredient-List Authority (Volume Implies Value) The $100M Swipe File (Schwartz) — list every component with explanations; exhaustiveness signals thoroughness
  • Social Proof Cascade (Largest Number First) The $100M Swipe File — lead with the most credible large number; cascade down through specifics
  • Multiple-Proof Stack (4+ Types of Evidence) The $100M Swipe File — testimonials, statistics, expert endorsement, and guarantees address different skepticism vectors simultaneously
  • Specificity Imperative (Zero Vague Language in Successful Ads) The $100M Swipe File — vagueness appears in none of the high-performing examples; every sentence should contain a number, name, or measurable fact
When to use Starting any ad from scratch. Use swipe file patterns as structural templates, then fill with Butler Button specifics. Especially useful for headlines, proof sections, and offer framing.

Specialized Categories & Digital
30 principles — Ogilvy, Bly, Confessions
marketing-digital-specialized
  • Tourism: Promote What Travelers Want, Not What You Prefer Ogilvy on Advertising — research the most-searched destinations; don't feature what the brand owner finds interesting
  • Email Subject Lines Follow the Same Rules as Headlines The Copywriter's Handbook, Bly — apply the 4 U's and 8 headline types to every subject line; 40-60 characters maximum
  • Landing Page = Web Sales Letter (8-Element Structure) The Copywriter's Handbook, Bly — headline, opening, problem, solution, proof, guarantee, CTA, P.S.; every element earns its place
  • Build the Email List First (Agora Model) The Copywriter's Handbook, Bly — the email list is the most valuable asset in online marketing; every page should offer a reason to subscribe
  • Press Releases Require Real News (Create Some if Needed) The Copywriter's Handbook, Bly — if there is no news, a proprietary survey or study creates it; launch itself is news
When to use Writing email campaigns, planning SEO content, drafting press releases, building landing pages, designing for B2B audiences, or adapting campaigns to international markets.

The Arsenal

The 6 Source Textbooks

1,206 pages fully read. 470 KB of structured extraction data. Every principle has a page-level source.

01
Ogilvy on Advertising
David Ogilvy
226 pages ~130 skills 99KB extracted

The broadest source: headlines, body copy, visual design, TV and radio, direct response, research methodology, corporate advertising, B2B, tourism, food, and pharmaceutical. Contributed heavily to Brand Strategy, Headline Writing, Visual Design, and Research disciplines. Ogilvy's empirical approach — every principle traceable to tested results — makes this the foundational reference.

02
Confessions of an Advertising Man
David Ogilvy (Chinese edition)
187 pages 100 skills 69KB extracted

Agency management, client relationships, hiring, brand culture, headline rules, body copy, illustration, and career ethics. Contributed primarily to Brand Strategy and Body Copy disciplines, particularly the honesty-as-strategy and respect-for-the-reader principles. The Chinese edition was read in full; all principles apply universally.

03
Effective Frequency
Michael J. Naples (foreword by Herbert Krugman)
150 pages ~55 skills 71KB extracted

The definitive treatment of exposure theory: how many times a prospect must see an ad before it produces action. Contributed entirely to the Frequency and Media Planning discipline. The 3-exposure threshold, reach vs. frequency tradeoffs, wearout theory, flighting strategy, and the Ebbinghaus forgetting curve all originate here.

04
The $100 Million Swipe File
Compiled from Hopkins, Collier, Caples, Ogilvy, Schwartz, Halbert
130 pages ~80 skills 58KB extracted

130 pages of annotated high-performing ads from the golden age of direct response. Contributed to Headline Writing, Body Copy, Swipe File Patterns, and Offer Architecture disciplines. The structural patterns (PAS, reader-as-hero, proof stacks, curiosity gaps) were extracted from observed frequency across the examples, not claimed theory.

05
The Copywriter's Handbook
Robert W. Bly (Portuguese edition)
307 pages ~70 skills 65KB extracted

The most systematic treatment of copywriting technique: 8 headline types, the 4 U's formula, AIDA, USP, BFD (Beliefs/Feelings/Desires), direct mail, brochures, press releases, TV, radio, web copy, email, SEO. Contributed to Headline Writing, Body Copy, Direct Response, and Digital disciplines. The Portuguese edition was read in full.

06
Secrets of Successful Direct Mail
Richard V. Benson
206 pages 178 skills 108KB extracted

The most principle-dense book in the arsenal: 178 discrete rules covering lists, offers, pricing, premiums, sweepstakes, testing, formats, envelopes, letters, renewals, conversion sequences, fundraising, catalogs, and full customer lifecycle. Contributed to Direct Response, Offer Architecture, Research and Testing, and Customer Lifecycle disciplines. The single largest source by principle count.

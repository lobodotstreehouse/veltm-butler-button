What Each Option Actually Delivers

👑

Butler Button — Human execution at everyday prices

Butler Button is the only service that combines AI-powered pre-trip planning with real human on-trip execution — including in-person errands and local ground support. Starting at $25/day with no membership, it's designed for travellers who want genuine concierge capability without paying $5,000+ a year for it. Pay only for the days you travel.

💎

Luxury Concierge Services — Powerful, but priced for the ultra-wealthy

Services like Ten Group, Quintessentially, and Knightsbridge Circle offer exceptional human support — but memberships typically start at $5,500/year and scale to tens of thousands. Most travellers simply don't travel enough to justify that cost. Their network is also primarily English and Western European, limiting effectiveness in Asia, Africa, and Latin America.

🤖

AI Trip Planners — Great for research, useless in a crisis

Tools like ChatGPT, Gemini, and dedicated travel AI apps can produce excellent itineraries. But they cannot call a restaurant, pick up your medication, rebook a missed flight, or physically be anywhere. When something goes wrong on trip — and it will — AI planners offer zero real-world execution. They're a starting point, not a safety net.

💳

Card Concierge — Inconsistent, narrow, and locked to a fee

Premium card concierge (Amex Platinum, Visa Infinite, etc.) sounds appealing, but in practice the service is uneven. It typically handles restaurant reservations and event tickets — nothing more. Response times vary wildly, language coverage is narrow, and the service is entirely conditional on holding an expensive card. No in-person support. No emergency capability.

Butler Button is the only affordable service that sends a human to physically handle things on your behalf. Lost luggage contents delivered to your hotel. Prescriptions collected from a local pharmacy. A birthday cake picked up and arranged in your room. That's not an AI feature. That's a Butler.
